A D U M B R A T E

ADUMBRATE is a program of United Nations which aims to restore the bodies of water back to beautiful and resourceful fjords. It aims to give anothe meaning to the word ADUMBRATE, which originally means to sketch out vaguely. The meaning of ADUMBRATE issued by United Nations is to stand up from ignorance to restore the bodies of water to what they once were.

LITTERING

Throwing garbage carelessly, also known as littering, is described to be one of the main factors as to why drainages, dams and resorvoirs are clogged in. It sheds light to issues such as intense flooding and big percentages of high tide in certain places.

ILLEGAL LOGGING

EXCESSIVE USE OF PLASTIC

With the recent rise of the usage of plastic in the society, it is no secret that these materials are thrown carelessly. You can easily spot plastics in any body of water by this time, with even the smallest water body, the stream, being clogged by plastics.

Why did United Nations launch ADUMBRATE? ADUMBRATE is focused on giving solutions to the maritime and fluvial issues of every country. Giving light to the excessive trash of every country to giving a new source of water for our brothers and sisters in Africa, ADUMBRATE aims to give solutions to the issues in the country of the Philippines as well : intense flooding and the abnormal rise of high tide caused by clogged waterways and depleted mountains and hills.

It is inevitable for the country of the Philippines to be hit by a storm, much less around the count of 20 storms per year. In a span of a year, Philippines welcomes almost 20 storms. This number varies year after year and these storms vary from sizes, speed, winds and after effects. The usual effect of storms are flooding and high tide in the calmer days after the storm. Families are forced to evacuate to safer and higher places whereas there are still people who refuse to leave their residences to protect their belongings.

The people residing in Menzyland, City of Malolos, Bulacan often experience intense flooding even after a rain shower. This is an effect of clogged drainages. These drainages are filled with tons of garbage. Due to the flooding, even the smallest means of transportation, the tricycles and pedicabs, have a hard time going to one place from another. There are schools near this place. These educational institutions are also affected, and so are the students of these schools. It becomes a hassle for students to attend school.

Leptospirosis is recently reported to be spreading once again! With floods becoming more and more evident as time passes by, cases of leptospirosis are spreading. Leptospirosis can be spread through open wounds that are exposed to floods, which are contiminated by rat fluid. Even if it is just small wound, it still matters because even the smallest wounds can kill you.
